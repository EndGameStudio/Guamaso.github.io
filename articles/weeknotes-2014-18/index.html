Never stop learning

I was watching ‘3 Days to Kill’ the other night. I’m a huge Luc Besson fan, ever since Leon (more widely known as ‘The Professional’). 3 Days to Kill was ok, it was entertaining. It has nothing to do with web development but there was a moment in the film that gave me pause regarding the progression of experiences and wisdom in our lives.

There’s a scene where Kevin Costner’s character is feeling really bad for himself, sitting on a bridge in Paris, eating Chinese food by himself. I thought, here’s this bad ass CIA guy who thought he had a solid grasp of his world. But in this moment, he knows nothing.

What I like about this movie is that it goes against the cliche, “You can’t teach an old dog new tricks”. Obviously, we get a happy ending for Kevin Costner after he learned to reassess what really was important in his life. There is a more important message being displayed which is, family is the most important thing. But let’s focus on the Old-dog-learns-new-trick aspect.

In the technology world, the most important skill to have is learning. Apparently, this goes double for professional hit-men/CIA/secret agent dads. But for people working with technology, there are moments where I get frustrated that I have to learn something new (it feels like very week these days). But once this new thing is learned and understood, I feel like He-man branding his sword high in the air; powerful and ready for the next challenge.

CanJS and Firebase

After building that quick CanJS page last week, I thought I’d take it a step further and build a CMS using Firebase as the backend. I previously had trouble getting CanJS to talk with Firebase using can.Model. After a few weeks of working at Bitovi and facing the different CanJS challenges, as well as reviewing the CanJS documentation, I was able to figure out a solution.

There aren’t any editing capabilities but the exciting part is it took me less than a day to build what I have so far. The next step is getting the live binding in CanJS to auto update the local Firebase data.

Goals for the week

I wasn’t able to spend a lot of time with Document JS, pretty much only what I need for work. I figured this will be an ongoing thing, learning Document JS as well as our other open source projects, specifically StealJS.
