Retro Lists

I looooveee lists. LOVE. It surprised me that I haven’t been keeping a list of sprint retrospective items the past few months. At the end of the sprint, I draw a blank for things we’ve done well and not-so-well.

This sprint, though, I kept a log. Stemming from my “Context Journal” (which is working GREAT, by the way), I decided to keep a page available in my notebook just for sprint notes.

It’s easy to keep a log of things that don’t go well. The hard part was recognizing things that went well (I think this is becauase when things go really well, they are actually invisible; we don’t know they go well because there wasn’t a problem standing out). But by keeping the sprint goals and overall project goals in mind, it helps to see positive trends and what causes them.

Either way, keeping a log or a list and removing it from your mind helps retain focus for what needs attention now - and it also helps the review process later!
